Why did you choose to major in Computer Engineering?

I have always loved technology. As a little kid I used to love to collect little gadgets and toys, and I guess that love carried over to technology as I grew older. I was always up to date with the newest technologies (I probably would have bought them, but I was a kid with no money). Being a pretty serious gamer, I always knew about the newest computer parts. I learned a lot of things in my quest to achieve optimal performance for my sub-par gaming rig, cause ya know, fps is a pretty big deal when you play first person shooters competitively. By the end of high school, I knew a lot different things about computers and generally how they worked, but I never got into the nitty gritty stuff like coding or learning how the different pieces of hardware actually worked.

Then came my Freshmen year in college when I was faced with the question that they say will determine the rest of your life, "What do you want to major in?" I was stumped and terrified. What happens if I make the wrong choice? At the very least I knew I wanted to do some form of engineering, as I had already applied and been accepted into the Clark School of Engineering prior to starting college, but I still couldn't make up my mind on which one. I was contemplating chemical, aerospace, and even material sciences. And then one day it dawned on me. I've spent my whole life around computers and technology, why not become a Computer Engineer. And so I did. Fast forward to present day, I honestly could not see myself doing anything else with my life. I love coding and finding solutions to problems.
